**Anticipated referee concerns for this type of paper:**

R1 — IDENTIFICATION:
- "How do you address the potential endogeneity of conflict to local economic conditions?"
  → Response: Village FE absorbs time-invariant confounders; province×year trends control for differential trajectories; conflict geography driven by pre-war ethnic composition (exogenous)

- "Could out-migration of certain types of people be affecting conflict intensity? (reverse causality)"
  → Response: Migration decisions by individual households unlikely to affect village-level conflict; conflict determined by armed group presence and military dynamics

R2 — SAMPLE / EXTERNAL VALIDITY:
- "The sample is small (872 households). Does this affect power?"
  → Response: Panel structure with 34,800 obs gives sufficient power; within-individual variation is the identifying variation; effect sizes are economically significant

- "How representative is the sample of rural Burundi?"
  → Response: HPS 1998 was designed as nationally representative rural survey; cite WorldBank 1998

R3 — MECHANISMS:
- "How do you distinguish migration for safety vs. economic reasons?"
  → Response: We do — that's the whole point of separating village violence (threat) from household victimization (economic loss); non-marital vs marital also distinguishes

R4 — LITERATURE:
- "How does this relate to X paper?"
  → Always check: Blattman & Miguel (2010), Mercier et al. (2020), Engel & Ibáñez (2007)
